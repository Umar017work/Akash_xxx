Principal's Message

Prof. Neelam Goel

Dear Students, Faculty, and Members of the College Community,

It gives me immense pride and joy to welcome you all to another promising academic year at Shyama Prasad Mukherji College for Women — an institution that has been nurturing excellence, integrity, and empowerment in young women since its inception in 1969.

At SPM, we believe that education is not merely about acquiring knowledge, but about developing the wisdom to apply it for the greater good. As Swami Vivekananda beautifully said, "Education is the manifestation of the perfection already in man." This belief guides our constant endeavour to help every student realize her inner potential and grow into a confident, compassionate, and responsible citizen.

In keeping with the vision of Dr. Shyama Prasad Mukherji, our college continues to uphold the ideals of nation-building through education, self-reliance, and character formation. His inspiring life reminds us that true learning blends intellectual pursuit with moral strength and social commitment.

Aligned with the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020, we are moving towards a holistic and multidisciplinary framework that promotes flexibility, creativity, and experiential learning. The NEP encourages our students to design their own academic pathways — fostering innovation, skill development, and adaptability in a rapidly changing world.

Dear students, I urge you to make the most of every opportunity this institution offers — be it academic excellence, research, cultural and social initiatives, or community engagement.

Prof. Neelam Goel

(Acting Principal) — Shyama Prasad Mukherji College for Women
Our Story

History of SPMC

Shyama Prasad Mukherji College for Women was established in 1969 as a constituent college of the University of Delhi. Named after the eminent Indian politician and statesman Dr. Shyama Prasad Mukherji, the college stands as a beacon of women's empowerment and academic excellence in the national capital.

Located in Punjabi Bagh (West), New Delhi, the college has grown from a small institution to one housing over 4,000 students across 19 departments, offering undergraduate programmes in Arts, Science, and Commerce.

The college has been NAAC Accredited and consistently ranks among the top women's colleges in Delhi University. It has a UDGAM Incubation Centre, a rich library, modern IT infrastructure, and a vibrant student community.

Over the decades, SPM has produced thousands of accomplished alumnae who have gone on to become professors, doctors, engineers, IAS officers, artists, entrepreneurs, and leaders in every field.

Our Namesake

Dr. Shyama Prasad Mukherji

Dr. Shyama Prasad Mukherji

1901 – 1953

Dr. Shyama Prasad Mookerjee (6 July 1901 – 23 June 1953) was an eminent Indian politician, barrister, and academic. He served as the Vice-Chancellor of Calcutta University (1934-1938) and was instrumental in laying the foundation of modern Indian educational institutions.

A staunch believer in women's education and empowerment, his legacy inspired the founding of this college. He was a founding member of the Bharatiya Jana Sangh and served as the Minister of Industry and Supply in the first cabinet of Independent India under Prime Minister Nehru.

His vision for education as a tool of national integration and individual self-realization continues to guide the ethos of SPM College to this day.

Vision & Mission

Vision

Mission

To provide quality education in a stimulating, inclusive, and technology-enabled environment; to foster critical thinking, research, creativity, ethical values, and a spirit of national service in every student.

Excellence · Integrity · Empowerment · Inclusivity · Innovation · Community Service · National Pride
